    • Welcome!

    I obtained a Ph.D in physical chemistry from the University of Arizona in fall 2013. My work involved laboratory gas-phase molecular spectroscopy on transient radicals, and observational molecular astrophysics using single dish radio telescopes to probe star forming regions and the envelope of evolved stars.

    Since April 2014 I am a NASA postodoctoral fellow at the Goddard Space Flight Center. My current research aims to better understand the origins of nitrogen isotopic variations in the interstellar medium, by investigating stellar nucleosynthesis as well as chemical and UV-induced fractionation effects.

    Recently, I discovered the world of machine learning and data science, and decided to embark on a journey to learn more about this fascinating field. This website is a collection of articles, thoughts and projects realized on the road to learning data science.
